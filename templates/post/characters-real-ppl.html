3 Comic Book Characters Based On Real People

April 10, 2019 By Anthony Gramuglia

John Constantine Is Sting

In an odd twist, John Constantine wasn't a character that was created then drawn to look like Sting. Rather, he was created because the artists wanted to draw Sting. Artists Steve Bissette and John Totleben asked Swamp Thing's then-writer Alan Moore to come up with a character they could draw to look like the pop music star. Moore decided that he wanted to create a younger, "blue-collar mystic" as most mages were commonly depicted as wizened, monk-like older men in most books. With that note, the artists drew up a streetwise Sting in a trenchcoat; thus, John Constantine was born.

J. Jonah Jameson Is Just A Cranky Stan Lee

Spider-Man co-creator Stan Lee wanted a newspaper editor character that was just a mean ol' cuss. He wanted this character to be a cantankerous jerk that would ride his employees, stiff them on pay, and be an overall obnoxious egotist. So naturally, Lee based the character J. Johah Jameson on himself. You can sort of see in Jameson's silver-sided hair and his mustache that his look reflected Stan Lee's appearance at the time. While he's seen as a genteel grandpa today, many workers at Marvel during Lee's 1960's run would agree that Lee was a slightly-toned down version of the Bugle's editor-in-chief.

Clark Kent Was Modeled After Harold Lloyd

Silent film comedy actor Harold Lloyd fits the Clark Kent persona perfectly. Lloyd's bespectacled characters would usually be timid, nervous, and awkward around women. Yet, much like Clark Kent himself, Lloyd would pull amazing daredevil stunts or impressive feats of strength in his films that would defy the conventions of his appearance, usually without the other characters around to notice. It's not unlike when Superman - still dressed as the mild-mannered Clark Kent - uses his Kryptonian powers when no one is around.
